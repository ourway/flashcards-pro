Mother's Day in the UK, celebrated in March, is all about showing appreciation for mothers and mother figures. Its roots are actually religious, linked to the medieval practice of visiting one's 'mother church' annually. Over time, this evolved into a secular holiday focused on family. The key thing to remember is that it's a personal, heartfelt celebration. While taking your mum out for a meal or to the cinema would be lovely, the *traditional* activity for children is giving gifts or cards. This is because even young children can participate in this way, often making cards at school or choosing small gifts to show their love and gratitude.
