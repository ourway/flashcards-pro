To vote in UK elections, the key thing you need to do is register to vote, which means putting your name on the electoral register. Think of it like this: the electoral register is the official list of everyone who's allowed to vote in a particular area. This system has evolved over centuries, moving from very limited suffrage to near-universal suffrage today. Registering ensures that only eligible people vote and helps prevent fraud. While things like paying taxes are important civic duties, they don't directly grant you the right to vote.
