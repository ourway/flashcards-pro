The Concorde, that iconic supersonic passenger jet, was a joint project representing a significant collaboration between Britain and France. Think of it as a symbol of post-war cooperation between these two major European powers. The project began in the 1960s, a time of great technological ambition. While other countries were involved in aviation and aerospace, the Concorde was specifically a British-French venture from its initial design to its eventual production and operation. The name itself reflects this partnership; "Concorde" means "agreement" or "harmony" in both English and French.
