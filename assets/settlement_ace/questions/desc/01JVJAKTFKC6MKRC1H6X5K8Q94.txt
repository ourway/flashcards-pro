The Elizabethan era, spanning from 1558 to 1603, marks the reign of Queen Elizabeth I and is often considered a golden age in English history. This period saw a remarkable flourishing of English culture and a growing sense of national identity. Think of Shakespeare, whose plays not only entertained but also explored English history and identity, fostering a sense of shared heritage. Exploration and maritime adventures, like those of Sir Francis Drake, further fueled national pride and expanded England's reach. This growing patriotism was a defining characteristic of the time. While the era wasn't without its challenges, such as religious tensions, the overall atmosphere was one of optimism and national self-confidence.
