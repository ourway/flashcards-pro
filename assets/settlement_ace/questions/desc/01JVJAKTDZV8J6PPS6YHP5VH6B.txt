Youth Courts in the UK deal with young people aged 10 to 17 who have been accused of committing a crime. The justice system recognizes that young people are still developing and deserve a chance to rehabilitate without their mistakes permanently damaging their future prospects. One key way this is achieved is by protecting their identity. Publishing the name or photograph of a young person accused of a crime could have serious consequences, impacting their education, employment, and social life long after the case is closed. Therefore, strict rules are in place to prevent the media from identifying them. This protection aims to reduce the stigma associated with criminal activity and give young offenders the best possible chance to turn their lives around. While transparency is important in the justice system, the welfare of young people is considered paramount in these cases.
