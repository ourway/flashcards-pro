Historically, the Home Office, headed by the Home Secretary, has evolved to manage a wide range of domestic affairs. The Home Secretary is responsible for setting policies, overseeing law enforcement agencies like the police, and managing the UK's borders and immigration system. So, when you think about who deals with crime and immigration, it's the Home Secretary whose primary responsibility it is. The Chancellor of the Exchequer handles financial matters, the Foreign Secretary deals with international relations, and other Secretaries of State have different departmental responsibilities.
