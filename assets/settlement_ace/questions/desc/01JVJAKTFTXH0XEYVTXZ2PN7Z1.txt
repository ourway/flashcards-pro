Wimbledon is synonymous with tennis, and it's important to understand why. The Wimbledon Championships are one of the four Grand Slam tennis tournaments, the others being the Australian Open, the French Open, and the US Open. What makes Wimbledon unique is its history and traditions. It's the oldest tennis tournament in the world, first held in 1877 at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club near Wimbledon, London. The tournament is played on grass courts, which is a surface now relatively rare in professional tennis, adding to its distinctiveness. While other sports are popular in the UK, Wimbledon is specifically a tennis event. The image of perfectly manicured grass courts, the all-white dress code for players, and the tradition of eating strawberries and cream all contribute to Wimbledon's identity as a premier tennis tournament.
