A meticulously hand-drawn, watercolor illustration in a realistic style, depicting a scene of immigration to Britain between 1870 and 1914. The central focus is a stylized yet representative depiction of a multitude of people disembarking from a ship onto British soil. The ship, rendered in muted browns and grays with subtle red accents, is anchored near the iconic white cliffs of Dover, painted in soft, pastel blues and creams. The immigrants, primarily Russian and Polish Jews, are illustrated with diverse features and clothing styles characteristic of the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The figures should be proportionally small, conveying the vast number of people arriving. A pathway or cobblestone street leads from the shore into a slightly visible, idealized British town in the background, with hints of Victorian architecture. The sky is rendered in calming pastel shades of light blue and white, with a few wispy clouds. The overall composition should evoke a sense of scale and movement, capturing the historical significance of this wave of immigration.
